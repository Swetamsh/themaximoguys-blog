MAS 9 is not a version upgrade. It's an architectural paradigm shift.

Say it again. Slowly.

When you upgraded from 7.5 to 7.6, you kept your WebSphere config. You kept your application server. You kept your database. You kept your customization approach. You kept your integration patterns. You applied patches to a familiar structure and moved on.

MAS 9 is not that.

The database model — containerized, managed differently.
The deployment model — Kubernetes operators, not installers.
The extension model — supported APIs and events, not direct code modification.
The integration model — event mesh and API gateway, not MIF-first.
The operations model — platform observability, not WebSphere thread dumps.

Every layer of the stack you're used to operating has changed.

Here's the one thing that transfers 1:1: your domain knowledge.

Work orders still work. Assets still work. PM still works. Workflow still works. The business logic of enterprise asset management — that's yours, and MAS respects it completely.

But the moment you plan your MAS adoption like it's a 7.6.1.x fixpack, you've already set yourself up for friction. Every team that has struggled with MAS did so because they expected an upgrade and got a platform adoption.

Every team that has thrived? They expected a platform adoption and planned accordingly.

Which camp are you in right now?
