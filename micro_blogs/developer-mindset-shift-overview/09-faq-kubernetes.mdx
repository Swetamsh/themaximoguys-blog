**"Do I need to learn Kubernetes to work with MAS?"**

It depends on your role -- and that's an honest answer, not a dodge.

If you're an application developer, you need minimal Kubernetes knowledge. Focus on APIs and extensibility patterns. Kubernetes will mostly be invisible to you.

If you're an integration developer, you should understand event-driven patterns and how services communicate, but deep Kubernetes expertise isn't required.

If you're an admin, you'll need the most exposure. But even then, focus on practical operations -- managing operators, reading pod logs, understanding resource limits -- rather than becoming a Kubernetes architect.

The short version: learn enough for your role, not everything.
