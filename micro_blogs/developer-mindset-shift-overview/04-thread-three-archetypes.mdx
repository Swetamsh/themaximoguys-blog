## Thread: 3 Maximo Developer Archetypes Facing MAS 9

---

**Tweet 1 — Hook**

You've spent years mastering Maximo 7.6. Now IBM says upgrade to MAS 9.

Except it's not an upgrade. It's an identity crisis.

Here are the 3 developer archetypes facing this shift — and what it actually means for each one.

A thread.

---

**Tweet 2 — The Application Developer**

The App Developer: You live in automation scripts, custom MBOs, and server-side logic.

Your fears: "Do my scripts survive? Do I need to learn K8s? What about my Java MBOs?"

Reality:
- Scripts largely survive
- MBOs need evaluation
- Kubernetes is mostly invisible to you
- GraphQL and REST APIs are your new best friends

---

**Tweet 3 — The Integration Developer**

The Integration Developer: You're the MIF master. Object structures, publish channels, enterprise services — in your sleep.

Your fears: "Do we start over? What happens to years of integration work?"

Reality:
- MIF still exists in Manage
- But it's no longer the center of the universe
- Event streams, App Connect, Kafka — welcome to the event-driven world
- Migrate incrementally. Don't rebuild everything at once.

---

**Tweet 4 — The Legacy Maximo Admin**

The Admin: You're the WebSphere whisperer. JVM tuning, thread dumps, DB2 query plans.

Your fears: "What happens to my job if IBM runs the platform?"

Reality:
- Your job doesn't disappear — it transforms
- Someone still needs to operate MAS inside your org
- Manage operators. Configure observability. Handle capacity planning.
- Your deep operational knowledge is invaluable.

---

**Tweet 5 — The Common Thread**

All three archetypes share the same new requirements:

- API-first thinking (APIs are the contract now)
- Event-driven architecture (react, don't poll)
- Observability over debugging (logs, metrics, traces)
- Infrastructure as Code (config is code)

The tools change. The engineering discipline deepens.

---

**Tweet 6 — The Reassurance**

Here's what nobody talks about enough:

Your domain knowledge transfers 100%.

Work orders. Assets. Preventive maintenance. Workflow. Inventory. Purchasing. Contracts.

All the same. All your expertise, fully intact.

What changes is the technology layer underneath those concepts. Not the concepts themselves.
