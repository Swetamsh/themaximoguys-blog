FAQ: "How long does the MAS 9 mindset shift actually take?"

The honest answer: 3 to 6 months. For experienced Maximo professionals who are learning deliberately.

Not 3 to 6 months to be dangerous. 3 to 6 months to feel genuinely comfortable — the kind of comfortable where you're reaching for the right pattern instinctively instead of stopping to think "wait, how does this work in MAS?"

That timeline assumes a specific approach. Deliberate learning, not passive exposure.

**What deliberate learning looks like:**

You're not just doing your existing job in a new environment. You're actually stopping to understand why the new patterns exist. Why is this an API call instead of a server-side method? Because APIs create clean contracts, enable diverse clients, and support versioning. OK — now you're thinking in MAS.

You're studying the architectural decisions, not just copying the syntax. The difference between someone who's been on MAS for 6 months and someone who's been on it for 12 but was just following instructions — it's enormous.

**What passive exposure looks like (and why it doesn't work):**

You migrate your 7.6 automation scripts line-by-line into MAS. They work. You never think about whether they should have been API calls. You batch-process your integrations the same way you always did. You call it done.

Six months later, you're on MAS but thinking in 7.6. Every upgrade cycle creates friction because you've built against the grain of the platform.

**The better question isn't "how long?" It's "how intentionally?"**

3 months of deliberate learning beats 2 years of passive exposure. The professionals who make the shift fastest are the ones who get curious about the why, not just the how.
