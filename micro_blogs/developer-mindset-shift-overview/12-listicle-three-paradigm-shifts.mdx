Three paradigm shifts that hit every Maximo professional moving to MAS, regardless of role:

**1. From "Modify" to "Extend"**
In 7.6, customization often meant changing IBM's code directly. In MAS, you build on top of the platform using supported extensibility points. Feels constraining at first. Leads to way easier upgrades.

**2. From "Scheduled" to "Reactive"**
The "run this CRON job nightly" mindset gives way to event-driven thinking. Instead of asking "when does this run?" you start asking "what event should trigger this?"

**3. From "Control" to "Collaborate"**
You no longer own the full stack. Feels like losing control. Actually, it's gaining capability. IBM handles platform patches. Red Hat manages Kubernetes. You focus on making Maximo deliver business value.

The common thread: your expertise isn't becoming less relevant. The way you apply it is evolving.
