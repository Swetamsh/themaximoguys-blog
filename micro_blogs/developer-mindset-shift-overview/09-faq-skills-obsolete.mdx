FAQ: "Will my Maximo 7.6 skills become obsolete with MAS 9?"

No. Full stop.

Let's be specific about what stays and what changes, because the fear here is usually driven by conflating the two.

**What stays — completely:**

Your understanding of the Maximo data model. Work orders, assets, locations, service addresses, PM records, job plans — all the same. Your knowledge of how workflow routing works, how escalations fire, how security groups control access — identical. Your hard-won understanding of how enterprise asset management works as a business process — that's the most valuable thing you have, and it doesn't touch the technology stack.

**What changes — genuinely:**

The HOW of extension. In 7.6, you modified. In MAS, you extend through supported patterns.

The HOW of integration. MIF still exists, but event-driven thinking becomes primary.

The HOW of operations. WebSphere is gone. OpenShift operators and Kubernetes are the new operational model.

The HOW of debugging. Thread dumps give way to distributed traces, pod logs, and observability dashboards.

The what doesn't change. The how does.

And on the how: most experienced Maximo professionals report 3 to 6 months to feel genuinely comfortable with MAS patterns. Not comfortable in "I can find my way around" — comfortable in "I know why these patterns exist and I'm using them naturally."

The key is deliberate learning. Not just doing the same things in a new environment. Actually understanding why the new patterns exist and building the muscle memory to use them instinctively.

Your 7.6 expertise is the starting line. MAS 9 is the next race. You're not starting over — you're leveling up.
