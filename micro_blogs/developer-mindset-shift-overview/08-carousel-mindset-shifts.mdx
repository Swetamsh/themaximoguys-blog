## Slide 1
**The Maximo Mindset Shift**
What changes when you move from 7.6 to MAS 9 -- and what doesn't.

## Slide 2
**Modify -> Extend**
7.6: Change IBM's code directly. Custom MBOs, JSP hacks, database triggers.
MAS: Build *on top of* the platform. Supported extensibility points only.

## Slide 3
**Server-Side -> API-First**
7.6: Direct SQL when you need it. Server-side object manipulation.
MAS: GraphQL and REST APIs are the contract. No exceptions.

## Slide 4
**Batch-First -> Event-Driven**
7.6: CRON jobs. Scheduled batch processing. Polling for changes.
MAS: Events trigger actions in real-time. Pub/sub. Webhooks.

## Slide 5
**Control -> Collaborate**
7.6: You own the full stack. WebSphere, database, OS, everything.
MAS: Shared responsibility. IBM handles platform. You focus on business value.

## Slide 6
**Debugging -> Observability**
7.6: Thread dumps. Server logs. Attach a debugger.
MAS: Aggregated logs. Prometheus metrics. Distributed traces.

## Slide 7
**What Stays the Same**
Work orders. Assets. PM. Workflow. Security groups. Inventory. Reporting.
Your domain expertise transfers 100%.

## Slide 8
**The Bottom Line**
It's not about forgetting what you know.
It's about rewiring *how* you apply it.
Your Maximo knowledge is the foundation. The technology layer evolves around it.
