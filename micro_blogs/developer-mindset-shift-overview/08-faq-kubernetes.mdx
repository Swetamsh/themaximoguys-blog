FAQ: "Do I need to learn Kubernetes to work with MAS 9?"

Direct answer: it depends on your role. And the dependency is smaller than you think.

**If you're an Application Developer:**

Kubernetes is mostly invisible to you. You write automation scripts. You call APIs. You publish events. The fact that your application runs on containers is infrastructure reality, not your daily concern. You don't need to write Kubernetes manifests. You don't need to understand pod scheduling. Focus on APIs and extensibility patterns — that's where your energy pays off.

**If you're an Integration Developer:**

You need to understand event-driven patterns more than Kubernetes itself. Know that systems communicate through events and APIs. Know that Kafka runs inside the platform. Understand the App Connect workflow model. The container orchestration layer? That's someone else's job.

**If you're a Legacy Admin:**

You need the most Kubernetes exposure of the three roles — but "most" is relative. You don't need to become a K8s platform engineer. You need to understand practical operations: reading pod logs, understanding operators, knowing what an OCP event means when something breaks. Think "Kubernetes user" not "Kubernetes administrator."

The analogy that helps us: you don't need to know how to build a car engine to drive to work. You do need to know that the check engine light matters and roughly what it means.

Kubernetes is the engine in this scenario. You're not rebuilding it. You're operating what runs on top of it.

The real skills to prioritize: API-first thinking, event-driven patterns, and observability tooling. Those will serve you regardless of the platform underneath.
