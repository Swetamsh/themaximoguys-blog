Counter-narrative incoming: your Maximo skills aren't obsolete. Not even close.

There's a fear running through the Maximo community right now. You can feel it in the forums, the LinkedIn comments, the conversations at conferences. People who've built careers on 7.6 expertise are wondering if MAS 9 makes them irrelevant.

It doesn't.

Here's what you know that doesn't expire:

Work orders and their lifecycle. Assets and locations and service addresses. Preventive maintenance and job plans. Condition-based workflow routing. Security groups and organizations. Inventory, purchasing, contracts. How PM generation works. How escalations fire. How to model a business process in Maximo so it actually makes sense to the people using it.

That knowledge? That's not attached to WebSphere. It's not attached to direct SQL access. It's not attached to WAR file deployments. It lives in your head and it transfers completely.

What changes is the technology layer underneath those concepts. Not the concepts themselves.

The developer who knows Maximo work order workflow cold is an enormous asset in an MAS 9 project — because understanding what you're building is the hard part. Learning GraphQL syntax is the easy part.

The admin who understands how Maximo handles concurrent users, what breaks under load, and how to tune for performance — that person is irreplaceable in a cloud migration. They know what questions to ask. They know what failure modes to watch for.

Your expertise doesn't expire. It becomes the foundation.

The technology layer shifts. You stay.

Now go learn GraphQL.
