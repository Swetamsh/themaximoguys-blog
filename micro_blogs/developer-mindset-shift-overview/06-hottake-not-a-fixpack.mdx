Hot take: Most MAS migrations are struggling because organizations are planning them wrong.

Stop planning your MAS migration like it's a 7.6.1.x fixpack. Start planning it like you're adopting a new platform — because you are.

Here's what fixpack planning looks like:

- Budget scoped for "upgrade effort"
- Timeline based on historical fixpack cycles
- Testing scoped to regression, not re-architecture
- Team composed of the same people doing the same jobs
- Success defined as "same functionality, new version"

Here's what platform adoption planning looks like:

- Budget scoped for enablement, training, and re-engineering
- Timeline that accounts for learning curves, not just technical tasks
- Testing that validates new extension patterns, not just old behavior
- Team that includes platform engineers, not just Maximo admins
- Success defined as "same business outcomes, better architecture"

The teams that budget for "an upgrade" and get "a platform adoption" end up in a painful middle ground. They're technically on MAS but still thinking in 7.6 patterns. They're fighting the platform instead of working with it.

Every direct SQL query that should be a GraphQL call is friction. Every batch job that should be an event handler is technical debt. Every customization that modifies instead of extending is an upgrade risk.

These aren't bugs. They're mindset mismatches.

The teams that thrive set the expectation upfront: this is a platform adoption. We are learning new patterns. We are re-evaluating our approaches. We are building for a cloud-native future.

That framing isn't pessimistic. It's honest — and it's the thing that sets successful teams apart from struggling ones.

How is your organization framing the MAS 9 move?
