**FAQ: What happens to my custom MBOs when we move to MAS 9?**

**Short answer:** It depends on the MBO. There are three categories and each gets a different answer.

**Category 1 — Simple extensions (usually migrate)**

Field defaults. Basic validation. Straightforward attribute overrides.

If your MBO does simple things in standard ways and does not reach deep into IBM's class hierarchy, migration risk is low. Test in a non-production environment and move forward.

**Category 2 — Complex business logic (evaluate carefully)**

MBOs with significant business logic, dependencies on IBM's internal APIs, or interactions with external systems need proper evaluation before migration.

The evaluation question: can this logic move to an automation script? Can it live as an external microservice? Is the coupling to Maximo so tight that it has to live inside Manage, or is that just how it was built in 2015 because that was the only option available?

Often the answer surprises you. The logic can move. It might even become cleaner.

**Category 3 — Deep framework hooks (likely redesign)**

MBOs that hook deeply into Maximo's framework internals, depend on WebSphere-specific behavior, or subclass IBM classes several layers deep — these are redesign conversations.

Not disaster. Redesign. There is a difference.

MAS 9 has extensibility patterns that handle most of what these MBOs were doing. The design changes. The business logic survives.

**The step you cannot skip:**

Inventory every custom MBO you own. Assign it a category. Document what business purpose it serves — not just what it technically does.

That inventory is your migration roadmap.
