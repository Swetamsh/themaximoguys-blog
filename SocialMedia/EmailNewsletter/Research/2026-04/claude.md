## 1. IBM TechXchange 2025 — Orlando Recap

**Dates & venue:** October 6–9, 2025, Orange County Convention Center, Orlando, FL. This was the all-in annual flagship; it replaced the old THINK/InterConnect model and is where IBM now centralizes Maximo product storytelling.

**What moved the needle for Maximo practitioners:**

- **"An Afternoon of Maximo"** on October 6 as part of IBM Community Day — FMMUG and MUWG co-hosted, Starboard Consulting facilitated. This is the free Community Day gateway session most practitioners attend before paid-conference days.
- **Maximo session track highlights** (per FMMUG/WMMUG session guides): *Real-Time Asset Insights with Maximo Health and Monitor (cable car operator case study)*, *Powering Agribusiness at Scale*, *Con Edison's AI-driven Digital Inspections with MVI*, and *From Reactive to Proactive: Unlocking Predictive Power with IBM MAS*. These reflect IBM's 2025 positioning pivot: Health/Monitor/Predict are the hero products, Manage is assumed.
- **Corporate-level announcement overlap with Maximo strategy:** the Anthropic–IBM strategic partnership (Claude embedded into IBM's software portfolio) and Project Bob were keynote-level. Second-order effect for Maximo: this is the foundation the Maximo Assistant / AI Service 9.1.x stack now sits on.
- **Session replays:** IBM confirmed in the Global AI community thread that *"TechXchange 2025 session files are available"* in the TechXchange Group library. Replays are gated behind IBM Community login, which is why practitioners often cannot find them via Google.

**Newsletter-usable links:**
- Conference hub: `https://www.ibm.com/community/ibm-techxchange-conference/`
- FMMUG session deck PDF: `https://fmmug.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/06/FMMUG2507_TechExchange_IBM.pdf`
- MaximoGroups.org TechXchange landing: `https://maximogroups.org/ibmtechxchange/`
- Session-files discussion thread: `https://community.ibm.com/community/user/discussion/ibm-techxchange-2025-session-files-are-available`

**Strategic read:** IBM used TechXchange 2025 to re-anchor Maximo as "AI-native APM," not "EAM with AI bolted on." Every case study on stage led with Health/Monitor/Predict, not with Manage. That framing will carry into 2026 product messaging.

## Strategic Summary (Three Moves Ahead)

1. **MAS 9.1 is now the platform story, not a release.** TRIRIGA → Real Estate & Facilities, AIP integrated, AI Service productized, Java 17 foundation. The 2026 newsletter should stop covering MAS 9.1 as a "new release" and start covering it as the default substrate. MAS 8.x coverage becomes a migration-planning beat, not a features beat.

2. **The content supply chain is bifurcating.** IBM-official blog output from Rachel Stein / Hideki Inoue is low-frequency (monthly at best). Partner content (Interloc, Naviam, Starboard, Banetti, Projetech) and user-group deck PDFs (SWMUG, PACMUG, LVMUG, FMMUG) are higher-frequency and often more detailed. A practitioner newsletter should treat these as first-class sources alongside ibm.com, not as supplements.

3. **Security bulletins are the highest-clickthrough item.** Seven CVEs in the last 12 months, with CVE-2026-4820 fresh as of early April 2026. A monthly "Maximo Security Watch" section anchored by IBM's bulletin page + Interloc's monthly roll-up would likely become the newsletter's lead engagement driver — outperforming product news.
