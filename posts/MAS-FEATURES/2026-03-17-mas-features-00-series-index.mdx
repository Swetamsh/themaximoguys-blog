# MAS FEATURES Series: What Changed from Maximo 7.6 to MAS 9

> **Who this is for:** Maximo administrators, developers, functional consultants, project managers, and business stakeholders navigating the upgrade from Maximo 7.6 to MAS 9 — and anyone who needs a clear, honest feature roadmap for what changed, what's new, and what broke.

**Estimated read time: 6 minutes**

---

## Why This Series Exists

You've been told MAS 9 is "the next version of Maximo." That's like saying a Tesla is "the next version of a horse-drawn carriage." Technically related. Functionally? A different universe.

The upgrade from Maximo 7.6 to MAS 9 is not a version bump. It's a **complete platform transformation** that touches every layer of the system — from the application server to the UI framework, from the mobile platform to the reporting engine, from the integration approach to the licensing model.

> *"So it's basically Maximo with a new look, right?"*

No. Absolutely not.

We wrote this series because every team we've worked with has the same reaction: they start the upgrade thinking it's a migration, and they finish it realizing it was a rebuild. The sooner your team understands the scope of what changed, the better your upgrade will go.

## Key Themes Across the Series

### 1. This Is NOT a Typical Upgrade
Every technical layer changed: deployment model, Java runtime, UI framework, navigation paradigm, mobile platform, integration approach, reporting engine, and licensing model. Your old muscle memory helps with business processes — but the mechanics are fundamentally different.

### 2. MAS 9 Is Not Just Manage
Organizations upgrading from 7.6 frequently treat it as a one-for-one replacement. It's not. MAS 9 is an **entire Application Suite** — Health, Predict, Monitor, Visual Inspection, AI Assist, Optimizer, and more. Your AppPoints license may already cover applications you've never explored.

### 3. Gaps Exist — And That's OK
Work Center replacements aren't 100% feature-complete. Purchasing RBAs don't exist yet. BIRT reports must be manually recreated in Cognos. Knowing the gaps upfront prevents mid-project surprises.

### 4. AI Is Now Native, Not Add-On
MAS 9.1 bakes AI directly into Manage — natural language queries, field value recommendations, similar record detection. The suite applications layer ML prediction, computer vision, and generative AI on top. This isn't a bolt-on; it's the platform's direction.

### 5. Data Quality Determines Everything
Every AI application downstream depends on the quality of your Manage data. If your install dates are 40% populated and your failure codes are inconsistent, Health scores won't make sense, Predict models won't train, and MRO Optimization won't optimize. Fix the data first.
